1 Anxiety as a Mood

1.1 Objects of my Anxiety

I often feel very anxious. The objects of my anxiety tend to fall under one of these categories:

  • work performance
  • social interactions
  • death or injury (of self or loved ones)
  • squandering my time (and thus my life)

1.2 The Moodiness

But the grounds for such anxious moods are not the object on which the anxiety focuses. It is rather a concern, a pervasive care, that enables a mood to arise and overcome our intention. As Heidegger notes in heidegger1967being (TODO ref), a frightening object can only appear as frightening because one is experiencing in the mood of fear. Thus, my anxious obsession over details from my work presuppose some basic concern, which makes the anxiety possible.

There may be any number of factors:

  • caffeine
  • lack of sleep
  • etc.

but even these can only catelyze anxiety because there is a structure of care that's makes the anxiousness a possible disposition.

2 Forgetting the Objective of the Work

I feed on praise and am terrified of being shamed, blamed, or otherwise denigrated. My work anxiety invariably stems from fear of blame, or a ravenous hunger for praise. 1

This has many problems:

  • is the source a valid/adequate judge?
  • even so, should I really be the one to bear blame or deserve the praise?

The more important point:

  • What is the point of the work?
  • is it to receive praise and aggrandize myself?
  • too bad if so
  • work worth doing should manifest a worthwhile change, whether that be an improved process, product, etc.
  • we should seek to work for the sake of good works and meaningful improvement
  • ideally, we would not be driven by pride and the pursuit of praise
  • but if we are going to succomb to that egoisitic motive, let it be pride in the product and result of the work itself
  • this focus can help us move out of task fulfillment into systems thinking and into being more impacful and effective in our work

3 Forgetting the Intersubjective Condition for the Work

I suspect every mood actualizes both revelations and velations: in the way things manifest under the guise of any mood, some dynamics are hidden and some shown.

Included among the things revealed by the tension between hunger for praise and fear of blame is the vital significance of intersubjectivity:

There's nothing more advanced than relating with others. There's nothing more advanced than communication—compassionate communication. (chödrön2005things)

Footnotes:

1

The dynamic of this particular fear/aversion is not the root of my work anxiety, but it is a powerful force discernible under the surface of every anxious episode. The question of the ultimate enabling condition for this disposition must remain open for the moment, as perhaps is should for all time. The attempt to deduce a single motive for a mood is probably a category mistake, and a misapplication of foundationalism.
